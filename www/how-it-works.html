How It Works

Bluesky Classifier analyzes public posts from Bluesky users to detect linguistic patterns associated with specific online subcultures. Our system uses a fine-tuned language model to provide accurate classifications, leveraging continuous scoring for nuanced, context-aware results.

Enter Username

Start by typing a valid Bluesky handle (e.g. username.bsky.social) into the input field on our homepage. Our system will verify the username format before proceeding.

Fetch & Analyze Posts

Using Bluesky's ATProto API, we securely fetch the user's most recent posts. These posts are then processed by our fine-tuned language model, which evaluates each post using continuous scoring to detect patterns associated with "weeb" and "furry" interests.

Classification Process

Our model assigns a primary and (if applicable) secondary classification to the user, based on the aggregate of post-level scores. The classification is determined by configurable thresholds for each category, allowing for nuanced distinctions such as "Slight Weeb" or "Slight Furry" in addition to strong signals.

Generate Results

The system displays the user's primary and secondary classifications, along with summary statistics and confidence scores for each category. This provides transparency into how the model arrived at its decision.

Understanding Classifications

Our system classifies users into three main categories with the following characteristics:

Weeb

Frequent references to anime, manga, Japanese culture, or specific terminology

Furry

Discussions around anthropomorphic characters, fursonas, or furry conventions

Normie

General content without strong signals from either specialized category

Bluesky Classifier uses machine learning to analyze user posts and detect patterns associated with furry and weeb subcultures. Results are based on algorithmic analysis and may not reflect a user's actual affiliations.
