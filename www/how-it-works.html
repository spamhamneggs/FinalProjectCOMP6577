How It Works

Bluesky Classifier analyzes public posts from Bluesky users to detect linguistic patterns associated with specific online subcultures. Our system uses a fine-tuned language model to provide accurate classifications, leveraging continuous scoring for nuanced, context-aware results.

Unlike simple rules-based systems, our model considers both the continuous scores (based on term matches) and the actual content/context of each post. The LLM is fine-tuned to use linguistic cues, context, and subtle signals—not just numeric thresholds—to make its predictions.

Enter Username

Start by typing a valid Bluesky handle (e.g. username.bsky.social) into the input field on our homepage. Our system will verify the username format before proceeding.

Fetch & Analyze Posts

Using Bluesky's ATProto API, we securely fetch the user's most recent posts. These posts are then processed by our fine-tuned language model, which evaluates each post using both continuous scoring (from term lists) and the post's actual language and context to detect patterns associated with "weeb" and "furry" interests.

Classification Process

Our model assigns a primary and (if applicable) secondary classification to the user, based on the aggregate of post-level scores and the model's understanding of post content. The classification is determined by configurable thresholds for each category, but the LLM can also use context and language cues for nuanced distinctions such as "Slight Weeb" or "Slight Furry" in addition to strong signals.

Generate Results

The system displays the user's primary and secondary classifications, along with summary statistics and confidence scores for each category. This provides transparency into how the model arrived at its decision.

Understanding Classifications

Our system classifies users into three main categories with the following characteristics.
Note: Classifications are not based solely on keyword counts or scores—the model uses both the scores and the actual language/context of posts for a more accurate, context-aware result.

Weeb

Frequent references to anime, manga, Japanese culture, or specific terminology

Furry

Discussions around anthropomorphic characters, fursonas, or furry conventions

Normie

General content without strong signals from either specialized category

Bluesky Classifier uses machine learning to analyze user posts and detect patterns associated with furry and weeb subcultures. Results are based on algorithmic analysis and may not reflect a user's actual affiliations.
